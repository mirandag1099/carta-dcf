# Carta DCF Valuation

**From a private company's financial statements to a discounted-cash-flow valuation, in seconds.**

🔗 **Live app: https://carta-dcf.vercel.app**

> _If Vercel assigned a different subdomain, update the link above to match your project's URL._

Drop a standardized annual-report PDF (or click **Try sample data**). The app deterministically extracts the historical income statement and balance sheet, you supply the handful of inputs that require judgment (forecast assumptions + cost of capital), and it produces a full DCF valuation with two terminal-value methods, data-quality checks, sensitivity analysis, and a one-click PDF export.

---

## What it does

A finance team completing the same valuation workbook for dozens of portfolio companies every quarter spends most of its time on the *mechanical* part — copying line items out of each company's statements and re-deriving the same formulas. This tool automates exactly that mechanical part while keeping the analyst in control of every judgment call:

- **Reads the PDF** and pulls the income statement + balance sheet line items deterministically (label + column matching — no AI, no API keys, nothing to break).
- **Validates the source data** (balance sheet ties out, margins reconcile, figures within plausible ranges) so you can trust it before it goes to a client.
- **Asks for the inputs that need judgment** — revenue growth, margins, CapEx/working-capital, and the WACC components — and keeps the valuation locked until you provide them.
- **Computes the DCF**: free-cash-flow forecast, WACC build-up, enterprise → equity bridge, two terminal-value methods (perpetuity growth and exit multiple), a sensitivity grid, and charts.

## How to use the platform

1. **Open the app** (link above).
2. **Load financials** one of two ways:
   - **Try sample data** — instantly loads the figures from the assessment Excel workbook and reproduces its valuation to the cent.
   - **Drop a PDF** — drag an annual-report PDF onto the dropzone (or click to choose one). Use one of the [sample reports](#sample-reports) to see it in action.
3. **Review what was extracted:**
   - **Validation & data quality** — green/amber/red checks (e.g. *Balance sheet balances*, *Gross margin reconciles with COGS*).
   - **Historical income statement** — the actuals pulled from the report.
   - **Balance sheet** — a collapsible card; expand it to verify the cash, debt, and equity that feed the valuation.
4. **Enter your forecast assumptions** in the left sidebar: revenue growth, gross / operating / EBITDA margins, CapEx % of revenue, and ΔNWC % of revenue. The forecast appears once all are filled in. _(D&A is derived from EBITDA − operating margin; tax rate is taken from the income statement.)_
5. **Enter your WACC inputs:** risk-free rate, levered beta, equity-risk premium, and pre-tax cost of debt (size/company-specific premiums optional). The **equity value** and the rest of the valuation unlock once these are in.
6. **Read the results** — equity value (two methods), WACC build-up, terminal-value comparison, charts, and a WACC × terminal-growth sensitivity table.
7. **Export to PDF** with the button in the header to share the dashboard.

> Nothing is assumed for you that shouldn't be: the report only provides *historical actuals*, and every forward-looking input is yours to enter.

## Sample reports

Five example PDFs live in [`public/samples/`](public/samples/) and double as the test fixtures for the extractor. They are **synthetic annual reports** — narrative pages (MD&A, risk factors) followed by a standardized income statement and balance sheet of historical actuals (2023–2025), matching the line-item structure of the assessment workbook.

| File | What it demonstrates |
| --- | --- |
| `sample-report-1.pdf` … `sample-report-4.pdf` | Four different companies with realistic, internally-consistent financials. Drop one to watch extraction + validation work end-to-end. |
| `sample-report-broken.pdf` | An **intentionally unbalanced** balance sheet — drop it to see the validation layer catch the error (the *Balance sheet balances* check fails). |

To use one: download it from the repo (or `public/samples/` if running locally) and drop it onto the app's upload area. They're regenerated by `scripts/make-samples.mjs`.

## A note on the numbers (two conventions)

- **Try sample data** reproduces the provided Excel workbook *exactly* — same mid-year discounting convention and prior-year equity bridge — so you can verify the model matches the spreadsheet to the cent.
- **Uploaded PDFs** use a standard **year-end** convention: the five forecast years are discounted back to the valuation date (end of the latest actual year), and the equity bridge / WACC weights use the latest actual balance sheet. So an uploaded report won't reproduce the Excel's figures — it applies the cleaner, financially-standard method to whatever statements you give it.

Extraction is fully deterministic and runs server-side; there are no external API calls or keys.
